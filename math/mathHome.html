Rate, Ratio, Proportion, and Percent

Rate, ratio, proportion, and percent are fundamental concepts in mathematics and statistics that help us understand and compare quantities.

  • A rate is a measure that involves dividing one quantity by another, often incorporating time as the denominator.
  • A ratio compares the relative size of two values, allowing for comparison across different units.
  • A proportion is a type of ratio that indicates how much of the whole is represented by the part.
  • A percent is a way to express a proportion as a number between 0 and 100.

Exponent laws

Exponent laws are mathematical rules that simplify expressions involving exponents. They include properties such as the product of powers, quotient of powers, power of a power, and power of a product, which help manipulate and solve equations involving exponential notation efficiently.
